# The Twelve — March 15, 2068

## The Climber 17 Incident

On March 15, 2068, a bootstrap climber carrying repair materials suffered a drive failure at 12,000 km altitude. The crew of twelve attempted emergency descent. The climber fell for 47 minutes before recovery systems failed completely. All twelve died.

"Remember the Twelve" persists as a slogan at labor gatherings. Their names are painted on walls throughout the Workers' Quarter.

---

## The Names

*Note: Labor history records their surnames. Full names reconstructed from personnel records.*

### 1. Antonio Reyes
- **Age at death:** 42
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Climber Pilot, Senior Grade
- **Family:** Survived by wife, two teenage sons in Iloilo
- **Notes:** Had piloted 200+ ascents without incident. His emergency protocols saved other crews; they couldn't save his.

### 2. Gregorio Buenaventura
- **Age at death:** 38
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Co-Pilot, Systems Specialist
- **Family:** Survived by wife, daughter in Cebu
- **Notes:** Had flagged intermittent drive warnings in the pre-flight check. Was overruled by schedule pressure.

### 3. Marisol Santos
- **Age at death:** 29
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Communications Officer
- **Family:** Survived by parents, siblings in Manila
- **Notes:** Her transmissions during the fall were recorded. She remained calm, reporting systems status until the end.

### 4. Benigno Aquino
- **Age at death:** 45
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Senior Structural Repair Technician
- **Family:** Survived by wife, three children
- **Notes:** Named after the martyred senator; became a martyr himself. Had trained two generations of repair crews.

### 5. Djoko Lukman
- **Age at death:** 36
- **Nationality:** Indonesian
- **Role:** Cargo Specialist
- **Family:** Survived by wife, son in Jakarta
- **Notes:** The repair materials he was carrying would have fixed the section of ribbon where four workers had died the previous month.

### 6. Bagus Wibowo
- **Age at death:** 33
- **Nationality:** Indonesian
- **Role:** Electrical Systems Technician
- **Family:** Survived by mother, siblings in Surabaya
- **Notes:** Had designed several of the safety interlocks. They worked perfectly—the drive failure was upstream of them.

### 7. Dewi Santoso
- **Age at death:** 27
- **Nationality:** Indonesian
- **Role:** Junior Pilot Trainee
- **Family:** Survived by parents in Yogyakarta
- **Notes:** On her fourth ascent. Would have received full pilot certification in two weeks.

### 8. Eko Pratama
- **Age at death:** 41
- **Nationality:** Indonesian
- **Role:** Emergency Response Coordinator
- **Family:** Survived by wife, two daughters
- **Notes:** Coordinated the crew's actions during the fall. His calm kept panic at bay.

### 9. Rosario Mendoza
- **Age at death:** 44
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Life Support Technician
- **Family:** Survived by husband, son in Davao
- **Notes:** The only woman on the crew. Had fought for her certification against resistance.

### 10. Juanito Cruz
- **Age at death:** 31
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Structural Repair Assistant
- **Family:** Survived by wife, infant daughter
- **Notes:** His daughter was three months old. She would grow up wearing his portrait on a pendant.

### 11. Eduardo Hernandez
- **Age at death:** 39
- **Nationality:** Filipino
- **Role:** Materials Handler
- **Family:** Survived by mother, four siblings
- **Notes:** Supported his entire extended family on his wages. The family received six months' pay as compensation.

### 12. Wahyu Suryadi
- **Age at death:** 35
- **Nationality:** Indonesian
- **Role:** Drive Systems Engineer
- **Family:** Survived by wife in Bandung
- **Notes:** Was attempting to repair the drive during the fall. His toolkit was found still attached to his body.

## The Response

After the disaster:
- High-altitude repair crews refused to ascend for three weeks
- The Consortium threatened contract termination and deportation
- Workers called the bluff—they were irreplaceable
- A safety review recommended 23 procedural changes
- The Consortium implemented 8 of them
- A blacklist of "troublemakers" was quietly established

---

## The Legacy

"Remember the Twelve" became the labor movement's rallying cry:
- Their names are invoked at the opening of every Union meeting
- Murals depicting the twelve faces appear throughout the Workers' Quarter
- The date—March 15—is observed annually with work stoppages
- Climber crews still say "for the twelve" before high-altitude ascents
